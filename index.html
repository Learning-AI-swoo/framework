AI in TCM: A Framework for Responsible Practice

Guiding the Future of TCM with AI.

To harness the potential of AI while upholding the core values of our profession, this framework provides the essential competencies for practitioners to engage with AI technologies ethically, responsibly, and effectively.

Five Core Domains

Five pillars for responsible AI engagement in Traditional Chinese Medicine.

Transparency & Explainability

  • ✓ Clearly disclose AI use in patient care, administration, or research.
  • ✓ Select AI tools that provide understandable and explainable outputs.
  • ✓ Communicate AI-generated insights to patients in accessible language.

Fairness & Inclusivity

  • ✓ Evaluate AI tools for potential biases affecting different populations.
  • ✓ Advocate for the use of diverse, representative datasets in AI development.
  • ✓ Recognize when AI outputs may disadvantage or exclude vulnerable groups.

Accountability & Responsibility

  • ✓ Maintain professional accountability for all decisions, even when using AI.
  • ✓ Differentiate between AI suggestions and personal clinical judgment.
  • ✓ Appropriately document AI use within patient or administrative records.

Risk Awareness & Mitigation

  • ✓ Apply basic risk assessment methods to evaluate AI tools before adoption.
  • ✓ Identify and respond to performance, safety, or privacy concerns.
  • ✓ Follow governance protocols for monitoring AI tool performance over time.

AI Capability Literacy

  • ✓ Explain fundamental AI concepts like machine learning and training data.
  • ✓ Critically evaluate AI-generated recommendations for accuracy and reliability.
  • ✓ Integrate AI information into workflows with appropriate human oversight.

Please Note

This framework provides guidance only and does not constitute direct education, training, or certification. It defines expected practitioner competencies to foster responsible AI awareness and use, without prescribing specific educational programs. This conceptual page was developed by the Education & Training subgroup of the NCCAOM AI Taskforce to illustrate the competency framework.
